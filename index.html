Research Identity

My research asks how physical AI systems can expose timing, uncertainty, infrastructure state, and experiment evidence as first-class system properties. Autonomous vehicles are my primary testbed because they stress perception, planning, control, networking, infrastructure, and reproducibility at the same time.

Collaboration Network

I have collaborated or worked with researchers and engineers connected to Autoware, Ford, Western Digital, BlueHalo, Leidos, the Federal Highway Administration, Oak Ridge National Laboratory, Tier IV, Toyota, and university collaborators around connected autonomy.

Current Faculty-Market Message

I am developing a systems lab agenda for physical intelligence: runtimes and operating systems for robots, programmable infrastructure for distributed physical AI, and digital twins that turn deployments into reproducible scenario evidence.
